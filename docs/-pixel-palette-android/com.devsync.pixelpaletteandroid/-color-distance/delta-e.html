deltaE

fun deltaE(color1: Triple<Double, Double, Double>, color2: Triple<Double, Double, Double>): Double

Calculates the CIE Delta E (ΔE) color difference between two colors in the CIE Lab color space.

The CIE Delta E (ΔE) formula calculates the perceptual difference between two colors in the CIE Lab color space, which is designed to be more aligned with human color perception. The Delta E value quantifies how different two colors are, and is based on the Euclidean distance between the two colors' coordinates in the Lab color space, calculated as:

ΔE = √((L1 - L2)² + (a1 - a2)² + (b1 - b2)²)

Where:

  • L1, a1, b1 are the CIE Lab components of the first color.

  • L2, a2, b2 are the CIE Lab components of the second color.

A smaller ΔE indicates that the two colors are perceptually similar, while a larger ΔE indicates a more noticeable difference between the colors. The Delta E scale can be used to assess the accuracy of color reproduction or to compare colors in design or imaging applications.

A ΔE of 1 is typically the smallest perceptible difference to the human eye, while a ΔE of 5 or more is usually noticeable to most people.

Return

A Double representing the Delta E (ΔE) color difference between the two colors. The smaller the value, the more similar the colors are perceptually.

Parameters

color1

The first color to compare, represented as a Triple of Lab values (L, a, b).

color2

The second color to compare, represented as a Triple of Lab values (L, a, b).

Throws

IllegalArgumentException

If the Lab components of the colors are outside valid ranges (L: 0-100, a: -128 to 127, b: -128 to 127).
